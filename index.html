Néctar

Illustration & Editorial

Néctar is a beekeping magazine.

The global population relies on bees to pollinate more than a third of its crops and beekeepers are necessary to ensure that there are enough bees around to do so.

The main goal was to combine delicate hand-made illustrations about bees, beekeping and honey with scientific articles that create an harmonius atmosphere through the pages, creating an appealing visual, because the content is as important as the form.

Rose Café

Branding & Packaging -

Rose café was born for the purpose of creating an unique experience, where you can savorate either an amazing thea, coffe or chocolate from our fair-trading partners.

But not only that. Rose café is not your next-door thea/coffee salon. Here you can engage in different workshops that are organize during the month and .

And if you don't have enough you can always take home those sweet memories that you will cherish by buying the products to enjoy them at home or the goodies. That's what we wanted to reflect with our branding work. The colorful of the experiences that we have having a coup of thea, keeping the different origines and cultures of the products and keeping their identity. And mostly, that fair-trade products don't have to be plain and boring, but they can be as appealing as the important message they want to share.

Time is Now

Branding - Visual identity

Time is Now is an non-gouvernmental environmental organisation which uses peaceful, creative confrontation to expose global environmental problems and the labels and companys that are participating to those environmental problems.

Their goal is to reach new generations and make them actors for the preservation of our world and the future.

We created the visual identity, the posters, stickers, the web page and the app for the organisation, as well as a flyer that can be ripped off with different images to create consciousness.

Les Misérables

Typography & Design

For the launch of the movie "Les Misérables", directed by Ladj Ly, we imagined an alternative poster for Utopia Cinema in Bordeaux.

For its released in Utopia Cinema, we worked on a combination of a poster with a brochure to give it to the audience. Mixing typography that created energy and movement with the red, blue and white that represents the french flag to symbolise the topic of the french society and it's layers that is adressed in the movie.

All this worked as an appetizer that created curiosity with images and visual typographic games, generating intrigue without making any "spoiler".
